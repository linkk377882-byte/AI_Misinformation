Detection Methods

With the maturity of large language models (LLMs) and diffusion models, false information generated by AI is evolving from simple text rumors to highly realistic visual and audio content with emotional manipulation. Effective testing is not only a technical challenge, but also a necessary means to maintain the ecological health of information. Today, with the rapid development of AI technology, the identification of false information is no longer based on intuition, but has evolved into a multi-dimensional technical system. We summarize the identification means into the following three core pillars, which together constitute a solid defense against AI misleading information.

01.

Algorithm Detector: Capture the “Fingerprint” of Statistics

Algorithm detection is the first filter to identify AI text. Its core principle is to use the machine learning model to analyze the statistical characteristics of the text, mainly focusing on two key indicators: Perplexity and Burstiness.

  • Perplexity: Measures the randomness of the text. LLMs usually predict the next word based on probability, so the text they generate tends to be too “smooth” and follow the most common probability distribution. Human writing is full of unexpected and low-probability vocabulary choices.
  • Burstiness: Measures the change of sentence structure. Human authors tend to alternate long and short sentences, with a strong sense of rhythm and obvious emotional fluctuations; while the content generated by AI often shows a high degree of uniformity and regularity in terms of sentence length and grammatical structure.

Through these mathematical indicators, tools such as GPTZero can quantify the “machine sense” behind a paragraph of text, so as to identify false manuscripts concocted by algorithms in a large amount of information.

02.

Digital Watermarking and Provenance: Digital Passport for Tracking Content

Unlike after-the-fact detection, digital watermark and traceability technology aims to solve the problem of identity authentication from the source. This is the mainstream direction promoted by technology giants and news organizations at present.

  • Digital watermark: This technology embeds invisible encrypted signals in its pixels (images) or bits (audio/text) when AI generates content. Even if the content is cropped or compressed, this “digital fingerprint” can still be extracted by specific software to prove its identity as a generative AI.
  • C2PA Agreement: As a frontier agreement proposed by the “Content Source and Authenticity Alliance,” C2PA is similar to issuing an “electronic passport” for each message. It records the metadata of the content — including who created or modified the content, when, and with what model. By checking the provenance of the content, users can clearly track whether a video was shot from a real camera or later synthesized by AI tools.
03.

Human Forensic Analysis: Insight into the Disharmony between Physics and Logic

Although the content generated by AI is increasingly realistic, it still leaves traces that cannot be concealed when dealing with complex physical common sense, light and shadow logic and language nuances.

Visual forensics: In AI images, human forensic experts usually pay attention to “physical consistency.” For example, is the projection direction of the background shadow consistent with the light source? Does mirror reflection conform to the optical principle? In addition, AI often distorts or blurs when dealing with fine anatomical structures (such as the number of human fingers, the shape of the auricle) and repetitive textures (such as dense fences).

Logic and context forensics: In long texts or dialogues, AI often shows “logical illusions.” They may state the facts in the first paragraph and draw completely contradictory conclusions in the third paragraph, or fabricate false references. Through in-depth verification of background knowledge, human readers can easily identify those pseudo-logics that “seem to be professional but are actually false,” which is an intuitive judgment that is still difficult to completely replace by technical tools.
